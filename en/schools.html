Exploring screen-free audio storytelling in education

This page provides context about an ongoing exploration into how audio and storytelling without screens can support listening, language development and autonomy in Early Years and early primary education.

My name is Alex Barrena. I work independently with schools and educational projects to explore simple, low-friction approaches to learning that fit naturally into classroom routines.

The current focus is on audio storytelling as a pedagogical tool that can be used during moments such as story time, listening corners, calm periods or transitions, without introducing screens or additional complexity for teachers.

This is not a sales initiative and does not involve promoting a specific product. The goal is purely to understand whether this type of approach is pedagogically relevant, and under what conditions it could make sense in practice.

What this exploration looks at

  • Listening and comprehension in Early Years
  • Language exposure and storytelling
  • Independent and calm learning moments
  • Screen-free classroom routines

I’m currently speaking with a small number of schools to exchange perspectives and learn from their experience. If this topic is relevant to your educational context, I’d be happy to briefly connect or exchange views.
